Introduction

This guide provides step-by-step instructions for installing fiber optic service in residential customer premises. Follow these guidelines to ensure proper installation and optimal performance of the fiber service.

Equipment Required

  • Fiber Optic Cable (Single-mode, Pre-terminated)
  • Optical Network Terminal (ONT)
  • Fiber Patch Panel (if applicable)
  • Fiber Cleaver
  • Fusion Splicer (if required)
  • Fiber Optic Power Meter
  • Safety Equipment (gloves, safety glasses)

Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Site Survey

    Assess the property to determine the optimal entry point for the fiber cable and the ideal location for the ONT installation.

    Important considerations for site survey:

    • Check for existing cable entry points
    • Identify the shortest path to customer's main telecom demarcation point
    • Consider customer's desired ONT location

    Fig 1: Example site survey diagram showing optimal cable routing

    Pro Tip

    When conducting a site survey, always use the Site Survey App to document entry points and take photos. This will help with any future service calls.

  2. Cable Installation

    Run the fiber optic cable from the demarcation point to the ONT location. Ensure proper cable management and minimum bend radius (typically 1.5 inches).

    Cable installation best practices:

    • Use cable staples every 12-18 inches
    • Maintain minimum 1.5 inch bend radius at all times
    • Label both ends of the cable
    Warning

    Exceeding the minimum bend radius can cause signal degradation or complete signal loss. Always verify your cable path before securing cables.

    Video: Proper Fiber Cable Installation Techniques (5:24)

  3. ONT Installation

    Mount the ONT in a dry, well-ventilated area near a power outlet. Typically mounted vertically on a wall.

  4. Fiber Termination

    Properly terminate the fiber cable using appropriate connectors (SC/APC is standard). Ensure clean termination to prevent signal loss.

  5. Power Connection

    Connect the power supply to the ONT and ensure it powers on correctly.

  6. Signal Testing

    Use the optical power meter to verify signal strength. Acceptable range is between -13 dBm and -25 dBm.

  7. Customer Equipment Setup

    Connect customer equipment (router, set-top box, etc.) to the appropriate ports on the ONT.

  8. Service Activation

    Contact the operations center to activate the service. Verify all services are functioning correctly.

Troubleshooting

No Light Signal at ONT

Check: Fiber connections, potential fiber damage, upstream signal issues.

Solution: Verify fiber connections are secure and clean. Test signal at demarcation point. If no signal there, contact operations.

Weak Signal Strength

Check: Fiber bends, dirty connectors, splice quality.

Solution: Ensure minimum bend radius is maintained. Clean connectors with appropriate tools. Re-splice if necessary.

ONT Not Powering On

Check: Power outlet, power adapter, ONT power connection.

Solution: Verify power outlet has electricity. Test power adapter with multimeter. Ensure connection to ONT is secure.
